My journey as a full-stack

When I first started writing code, I was fascinated by how a few lines of logic could transform into something useful and interactive.

What does being a full-stack mean

full-stack developer is someone who can work on both the front-end and back-end of a web application. That means not just making things look good in the browser, but also ensuring they actually work under the hood.

Why I love full-stack devlopment

Working across the stack allows me to see the bigger picture. I enjoy designing APIs, managing databases, and optimizing performance just as much as I love crafting clean, intuitive user interfaces.

My stack of choice

Frontend: HTML,CSS, JavaScript,Tailwind,React Backend: Node.Js, express. Databases: Mongo DB. Control Version: Git, GitHub.

Recent Projects I'm Proud Of

I’ve worked on everything from dashboards and portfolio websites to full SaaS platforms. One of my favorite projects was a job-tracking web app that helped users keep track of their applications, interviews.

My Design Philosophy

I believe good design starts with empathy. Every project begins by asking: Who am I designing for, and what do they need? From there, I focus on crafting solutions that are not only visually appealing but also intuitive, accessible, and purposeful.

My Toolbox

UI/UX Design: Figma, Adobe XD, Sketch Visual Design: Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, After Effects Prototyping & Interaction: Framer, InVision Design Principles: Accessibility, responsiveness, human-centered design
